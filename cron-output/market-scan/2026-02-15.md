## Executive Summary

The AI agent economy is experiencing a fundamental business model transformation in early 2026. We're witnessing a decisive shift from **input-based pricing** (seats, API calls) to **outcome-based value capture** (resolved tickets, booked meetings, completed workflows). This represents the most significant pricing paradigm shift in enterprise software since the move from perpetual licenses to SaaS subscriptions.

Key inflection point: **40% of enterprise SaaS is expected to include outcome-based pricing elements by end of 2026**, up from just 15% two years prior (Gartner). Companies that master this transition are commanding 100x+ ARR multiples, while those clinging to traditional models face compression.

### 4. Hybrid Pricing with "Burstable Reserve" Models

**Category:** AI Infrastructure / SaaS Pricing Innovation

**Business Model:** Combines predictable base pricing with outcome-based pricing for usage spikes. Example: Fixed price for 1,000 monthly resolutions, then per-success fee beyond that. Balances budget predictability with value alignment.

**Why It Matters:**
- **Solves the predictability problem:** CFOs hate purely variable cost structures
- **Smooths vendor revenue:** Avoids feast-or-famine of pure outcome models
- **Reduces buyer hesitation:** "Safe" base tier with upside participation
- **Captures heavy users:** High-volume customers pay for value without sticker shock

**Key Players:**
- **Chargebee:** Infrastructure enabling hybrid models for AI SaaS
- **Orb:** Billing platform supporting outcome-based pricing implementation
- **Most AI-native SaaS:** Moving to base subscription + usage/outcome overage

**Investment Implications:**
- Billing infrastructure plays (Stripe, Chargebee, Orb) benefit from pricing model complexity
- Companies with mature data (5+ years) can better predict outcomes and price confidently
- Early-stage startups should avoid pure outcome pricing until they have outcome predictability
