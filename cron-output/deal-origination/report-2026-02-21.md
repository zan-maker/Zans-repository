## Executive Summary

The blue-collar business acquisition market remains exceptionally active in Q1 2026. Private equity firms have acquired approximately **800 HVAC, plumbing, and electrical businesses since 2022**, with 2024 marking a record year for transaction volume. Activity has continued steadily into 2025-2026.

**Key Market Dynamics:**
- Strategic buyer dominance is shifting: PE accounted for 50.6% of transactions in 2025 vs 32.9% prior year
- Roll-up strategies are the dominant play: Platform + tuck-in acquisitions
- Founder-owned businesses ($2-15M revenue) are primary targets
- Geographic consolidation in high-density metro areas is the priority
- Recurring maintenance revenue is the holy grail metric

## SECTION 5: DEAL FLOW SOURCING STRATEGY

### Tactics for Off-Market Discovery:

**1. Association Mining**
- ACCA (Air Conditioning Contractors of America)
- PHCC (Plumbing-Heating-Cooling Contractors)
- IEC (Independent Electrical Contractors)
- ABC (Associated Builders and Contractors)
- Local Chamber trade directories

**2. Vendor Channel Partnerships**
- Lennox, Carrier, Trane distributors know who's selling
- Ferguson Plumbing supply relationships
- Johnstone Supply regional managers
- Local equipment finance companies

**3. Geographic Clustering**
- Target 3-5 metro areas for density
- Map competitors in each market
- Identify the "last independent" in PE-heavy markets
- Focus on markets with 2-3 PE platforms already present

**4. Digital Signals**
- LinkedIn: "Open to opportunities" + HVAC owner titles
- Google Business Profile: Reduced posting activity
- Website: "Under new management" (recent exits)
- Job boards: Hiring slowdown (exit prep)

**5. Direct Mail Campaigns**
- Target: Aged business owners (60+)
- Message: Succession planning, legacy preservation
- Response rate: 2-5% in trades (higher than average)

## SECTION 7: RISK FACTORS & CONSIDERATIONS

### Market Risks:
1. **Labor Shortage:** HVAC tech shortage persists; verify team stability
2. **Interest Rate Sensitivity:** Rising rates compress multiples
3. **PE Saturation:** Some markets over-consolidated; check competitive density
4. **Regulatory:** EPA refrigerant changes (2025) impact equipment sales

### Due Diligence Red Flags:
- Owner IS the lead technician
- >40% revenue from new construction
- Customer concentration >20%
- Unresolved warranty claims
- Cash business (minimal financial records)
