### Box 2 — What It Means for Fractional CFO Outreach

**Sapiom Analysis:**
- **Why it fits ICP:** Seed-stage, just raised capital, B2B infrastructure (complex financial operations), top-tier investors create reporting expectations
- **CEO background signal:** Ex-Shopify payments director likely understands value of financial infrastructure
- **Asymmetric risk:** If they already have CFO plans, outreach wasted. If they don't, early relationship valuable.
- **Scenario modeling:**
  - Base case: 30% response rate, 10% meeting rate (fresh funding attention)
  - Upside: Seed-stage companies often delay CFO hire; high receptiveness
  - Downside: Accel may provide portfolio CFO resources

**Duna Analysis:**
- **Why it fits ICP:** Series A stage, multi-entity operations (Germany + Netherlands), fintech (complex compliance), enterprise customers
- **Founder background signal:** Stripe alumni likely have high bar for financial operations
- **Geographic complexity:** Dual HQ suggests multi-entity financial needs, but European location may preference local CFO
- **Scenario modeling:**
  - Base case: 20% response rate (European company may prefer local)
  - Upside: CapitalG investment suggests US market interest; founder US network
  - Downside: European operations may require EU-based CFO

**Fibr AI Analysis:**
- **Why it fits ICP:** Accel-backed (high-growth expectations), enterprise sales (complex contracts), seed-stage transitioning to scale
- **Concern:** Lower funding amount ($5.7M) may not prioritize CFO spend yet
- **Scenario modeling:**
  - Base case: 25% response rate
  - Upside: Enterprise customers with 3-5 year contracts = immediate need for contract/renewal management
  - Downside: Earlier stage = may defer CFO decision
