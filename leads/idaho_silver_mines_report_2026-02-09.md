## Executive Summary

Idaho's Silver Valley (Coeur d'Alene Mining District) in Shoshone County is one of the most historically significant silver mining regions in the United States. While many historic mines have closed, **Hecla Mining Company** remains the dominant active operator in the region.

**Key Finding:** Only **1 active major silver mine** currently operating in Idaho, with several historic mines in various stages of closure, care & maintenance, or redevelopment.

## Silver Valley (Coeur d'Alene Mining District) Overview

### Geography
- **Location:** Northern Idaho Panhandle, Coeur d'Alene Mountains
- **Counties:** Shoshone County (primary), Kootenai County
- **Length:** ~40 miles (64 km)
- **Towns:** Wallace, Kellogg, Mullan, Wardner, Osburn, Silverton, Pinehurst, Smelterville

### Historic Significance
- Dating back to the 1880s
- One of the world's richest silver districts
- Total historic production: **1.2+ billion ounces of silver**
- Designated Superfund site in 1983 due to contamination

### Geology
- Proterozoic metasediments (Belt series)
- Polymetallic veins of hydrothermal origin
- Primary formations: Prichard, Burke, Revett, St. Regis, Wallace
